# Detailed Notes

## AYCE GOGI

- All-you-can-eat Korean BBQ in Van Nuys
- Great selection of pinball machines (make sure to check out the backroom!)

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=AYCE+GOGI+Van+Nuys+CA)

## Birria and Pozole To Go

- To-go only (no dine-in), in Woodland Hills
- Excellent red pozole; solid birria tacos
- Cash, Apple Pay, or Zelle only — no cards

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Birria+and+Pozole+To+Go+Woodland+Hills+CA)

## Blue Note Hollywood

Jazz club in Hollywood.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Blue+Note+Hollywood+CA)

## Bridge to Nowhere

- Popular hike along the East Fork of the San Gabriel River, ending at a 1930s arch bridge that was never connected to a road. 
- Several river crossings involved.
- Use caution on hot days, bring enough water.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Bridge+to+Nowhere+hike+Azusa+CA)

## Caltech

Walk the campus of Caltech (California Institute of Technology).

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Caltech+Pasadena+CA)

## Cara

Glamorous old Hollywood-style cocktail bar and restaurant at the Cara Hotel in Los Feliz. Great for drinks with live music around 5pm. Also serves brunch, lunch, and dinner on an outdoor patio.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Cara+Hotel+Los+Feliz+Los+Angeles+CA)

## Castaway Burbank

Upscale hilltop restaurant in Burbank with panoramic views of the San Fernando Valley and city lights. Steak-forward menu with dry-aged cuts, a raw bar, charcuterie, and a floor-to-ceiling wine cellar. Reservations recommended.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Castaway+Burbank+CA)

## Chilenazo

Small, informal Chilean spot in Canoga Park. Excellent empanadas; also does churrascos, completos, and pastries. Very affordable.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Chilenazo+Canoga+Park+Los+Angeles+CA)

## Colorado Street Bridge

Historic 1913 concrete arch bridge over the Arroyo Seco with scenic views.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Colorado+Street+Bridge+Pasadena+CA)

## Destroyer

Counter-service brunch spot by chef Jordan Kahn in Culver City. Scandinavian-influenced New American dishes. Trendy and distinctive.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Destroyer+Culver+City+CA)

## Echo Park Time Travel Mart

Quirky "convenience store for time travelers" at 1714 W Sunset Blvd in Echo Park. Actually a front for 826LA, a literary nonprofit offering free tutoring and creative writing to kids ages 6–18 — all store proceeds benefit the program. Browse wares like Barbarian Repellent and Robot Emotions. Free to enter.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Echo+Park+Time+Travel+Mart+Los+Angeles+CA)

## EightyTwo LA

Bar and arcade in DTLA. 

- Great selection of pinball machines
- Decent beer and cocktails
- Loud
- Attracts a mixed (younger) crowd, security will search/scan you for weapons

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=EightyTwo+LA+DTLA)

## Fork in the Road

Giant 18-foot wooden fork sculpture at the spot where Pasadena Avenue splits — a quirky local landmark.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Fork+in+the+Road+Pasadena+CA)

## Franklin D. Murphy Sculpture Garden

Free, five-acre outdoor sculpture garden on the UCLA campus with over 70 works by Rodin, Calder, Henry Moore, Barbara Hepworth, and others. Peaceful and underrated. No ticket or reservation needed.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Franklin+D+Murphy+Sculpture+Garden+UCLA+CA)

## Gamble House

1908 Arts and Crafts masterpiece by architects Greene & Greene, built for the Gamble family (of Procter & Gamble fame). One-hour docent-led tours Thu–Sun, noon to 3pm (~$17/adult). Reservations recommended — tours sell out.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Gamble+House+Pasadena+CA) · [Website](https://gamblehouse.org/)

## Geoffrey's Malibu

Upscale oceanfront restaurant on PCH with ocean views. Food is solid (not great), but the
views are incredible and it draws a quirky LA crowd. Reservations recommmended.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Geoffrey%27s+Malibu+CA)

## Getty Villa

Museum of ancient Greek, Roman, and Etruscan antiquities in a villa setting on PCH. Timed-entry tickets required (free).

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Getty+Villa+Pacific+Palisades+CA)

## Go's Mart

Hidden gem sushi bar in Canoga Park run by one chef. No physical menu — just a whiteboard of the day's catch. Omakase experience; reservations required. Expensive.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Go%27s+Mart+Canoga+Park+Los+Angeles+CA)

## Griffith Observatory

Iconic art deco observatory in Griffith Park with panoramic views of LA and the Hollywood Sign. Grounds are free; planetarium shows cost extra.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Griffith+Observatory+Los+Angeles+CA)

## Hollywood Bowl

Iconic outdoor amphitheater in the Hollywood Hills. Great for summer concerts; bring a picnic.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hollywood+Bowl+Los+Angeles+CA)

## Hollywood Forever Cemetery

Active cemetery and LA cultural landmark. Cinespia hosts outdoor movie screenings here on summer weekend nights — bring a picnic blanket, food, and drinks and watch a film projected onto a mausoleum wall. Check cinespia.org for the schedule and tickets (~$30); screenings sell out. Gorgeous and memorable setting.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hollywood+Forever+Cemetery+Los+Angeles+CA) · [Website](https://cinespia.org/)

## Huntington Gardens

World-class botanical gardens, art galleries (including Gainsborough's Blue Boy), and old books.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=The+Huntington+San+Marino+CA)

## Inn of the Seventh Ray

Romantic outdoor creekside restaurant set in Topanga Canyon, with organic and locally sourced New American cuisine. Stunning natural setting with a stream running alongside the dining area. Reservations recommended.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Inn+of+the+Seventh+Ray+Topanga+CA)

## Jumbo's Clown Room

Iconic dive bar and burlesque venue in Hollywood.

- **WARNING**: Probably draws an intersting crowd, might be a bit on the sketchy side

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Jumbo%27s+Clown+Room+Hollywood+CA)

## Maru Espresso Bar

Fancy espresso bar. Long line, very little seating, no bathroom. But it has good drinks
and great people (and dog) watching.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Maru+Espresso+Bar+Beverly+Hills+CA)

## Mulholland Drive

Scenic road through the Hollywood Hills and Santa Monica Mountains with sweeping views of LA and the valley.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Mulholland+Drive+Los+Angeles+CA)

## Pizzeria Mozza

LA institution for wood-fired Neapolitan-style pizza, co-owned by Nancy Silverton. At 641 N Highland Ave near Melrose. Expect long waits to be seated.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Pizzeria+Mozza+Los+Angeles+CA)

## Point Dume

Coastal headland in Malibu with hiking, ocean views, and tide pools. Classic Malibu.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Point+Dume+Malibu+CA)

## Porto's Bakery

Beloved Cuban bakery. Famous for croquetas, potato balls, and pastries. Long lines, good value.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Porto%27s+Bakery+Burbank+CA)

## Rodeo Drive

Famous luxury shopping street in Beverly Hills.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Rodeo+Drive+Beverly+Hills+CA)

## Santa Monica Boardwalk

The Santa Monica Pier and beachfront boardwalk, with Pacific Park amusement rides and street performers.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Santa+Monica+Pier+CA)

## Seven Grand

Whiskey bar in DTLA.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Seven+Grand+DTLA+Los+Angeles+CA)

## Space Shuttle Endeavour

Space Shuttle Endeavour on display at the California Science Center in Exposition Park.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=California+Science+Center+Los+Angeles+CA)

## Sugarfish

Popular sushi chain with high-quality set menus for a reasonable price.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Sugarfish+Los+Angeles+CA)

## Tacos Reyes

Canoga Park taqueria at 21929 Saticoy St. Excellent shrimp burrito.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Tacos+Reyes+Canoga+Park+CA)

## The Greek Theatre

Historic 5,900-seat outdoor amphitheater in Griffith Park, one of LA's best summer concert venues. Open since 1930. Great sightlines and warm evenings. Bring a light jacket — it cools down after sunset.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Greek+Theatre+Los+Angeles+CA) · [Website](https://www.lagreektheatre.com/)

## The Magic Castle

Legendary private clubhouse for the Academy of Magical Arts, open only to members and their guests. Absolutely worth the effort to get in.

- Three ways in: 
  1. Know a member magician (ask them to add you as a guest)
  2. Call the hostess line directly (miracles happen)
  3. Book a stay at the Hollywood Roosevelt Hotel (which includes access)
- Dinner reservation required (~$75+ per person minimum). 
- Dress code strictly enforced — no denim. 

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=The+Magic+Castle+Hollywood+CA)

## Universal City Walk

Free open-air entertainment complex attached to Universal Studios Hollywood. Restaurants, bars, shops, and live music — good for a casual evening out without paying park admission.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Universal+City+Walk+Los+Angeles+CA)

## Universal Studios Hollywood

Hollywood movie studio theme park with the iconic backlot tram tour and rides based on Universal franchises (Jurassic World, Harry Potter, Transformers, etc.). Tickets run $109–$154+ depending on date; buy online in advance.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Universal+Studios+Hollywood+CA) · [Website](https://www.universalstudioshollywood.com/)

## Venice Beach Boardwalk

Two-mile oceanfront promenade with street performers, vendors, the Venice Art Walls, Muscle Beach, and a skatepark. Historically iconic — a backdrop for countless LA films. Worth a daytime stroll; the area has grown grittier in recent years. Go during daylight.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Venice+Beach+Boardwalk+Los+Angeles+CA)

## Venice Canals

Six-canal historic district a few blocks from Venice Beach. A tranquil walk through a charming residential neighborhood. A little touristy but still worth it.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Venice+Canals+Los+Angeles+CA)

## Westfield Century City

Massive upscale outdoor mall on the Westside anchored by Nordstrom, Bloomingdale's, and Eataly. Good for people-watching and the Hollywood/LA scene. AMC Theatres on-site.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Westfield+Century+City+Los+Angeles+CA)

## Will Geer Theatricum Botanicum

- Charming professional outdoor theater in Topanga Canyon, with lots of Shakespeare works.
- Parking is along the canyon road, which can be annoying and dangerous for pederstrians... **always walk inside of a path not on the main road facing cars passing**!

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Will+Geer+Theatricum+Botanicum+Topanga+CA)

## Zankou Chicken

- Armenian Lebanese rotisserie chicken chain; multiple LA locations
- Avoid the Hollywood location
- Famous for the garlic paste
- Beef shawarma and chicken tarna plates are great

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Zankou+Chicken+Los+Angeles+CA)

## Zuma Beach

Wide sandy beach in Malibu, one of LA's best for swimming. Can have epic sunsets.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Zuma+Beach+Malibu+CA)
