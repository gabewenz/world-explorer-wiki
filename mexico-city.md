# Detailed Notes

## Pujol

Enrique Olvera's flagship; tasting menu or taco omakase bar rooted in traditional Mexican cuisine. Two Michelin stars, perennial World's 50 Best. Signature mole madre, mole nuevo (aged black mole reheated thousands of times). Reservations well in advance.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Pujol+Mexico+City) · [Website](https://pujol.com.mx/)

## Quintonil

Jorge Vallejo's contemporary Mexican spot; ~98% Mexican ingredients (nopal, quintonil, huauzontle), tasting menu. Two Michelin stars; ranked #3 on World's 50 Best 2025. Book well ahead.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Quintonil+Mexico+City) · [Website](https://quintonil.com/)

## Rosetta

Elena Reygadas's restaurant in a Porfirian mansion; Mexican cuisine with Italian/Mediterranean influence, plant-forward, seasonal à la carte for sharing. One Michelin star; Reygadas named World's Best Female Chef 2023. Don't miss the adjacent Panadería Rosetta bakery.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Rosetta+Colima+166+Roma+Norte+Mexico+City) · [Website](https://rosetta.com.mx/en)

## Blanco Colima

Contemporary Mexican/international in a restored Porfirian mansion on Colima St; elegant courtyard setting, good for a dressier dinner.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Blanco+Colima+Roma+Norte+Mexico+City) · [Website](https://www.blancocolimamx.com/)

## Altanera

Modern Mexican with a Baja/seafood lean — aguachiles, shrimp tacos, and adventurous bites. Lively, casual.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Altanera+Roma+Mexico+City)

## Gardela

Argentine-Italian steakhouse on Álvaro Obregón; dry-aged steaks, pastas, a raw bar, and a 100+ label wine list. Casual-elegant.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Gardela+Roma+Norte+Mexico+City)

## Marcello

Italian spot in La Roma with retro 1960s decor; handmade pastas, pizzas, and a curated wine list.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Marcello+Roma+Mexico+City)

## Castizo

Contemporary Spanish and Mediterranean cuisine; good service, polished plates. (Some listings place a location in Condesa/Hipódromo — confirm the branch.)

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Castizo+Roma+Mexico+City)

## Centro Histórico

Historic core of CDMX (UNESCO World Heritage). Zócalo main square, Metropolitan Cathedral, Templo Mayor Aztec ruins, and Palacio Nacional with Diego Rivera murals. Walkable; combine with Bellas Artes.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Centro+Histórico+Mexico+City)

## Palacio de Bellas Artes

Iconic Art Nouveau/Art Deco performing-arts palace; murals by Rivera, Orozco, Siqueiros and the Museo del Palacio inside. Home of the Ballet Folklórico. Building exterior free; museum/theater ticketed. View it from the Sears café terrace across the street.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Palacio+de+Bellas+Artes+Mexico+City)

## Museo Nacional de Antropología

World-class anthropology museum in Bosque de Chapultepec; vast collection of Mesoamerican artifacts including the Aztec Sun Stone. Plan several hours.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Museo+Nacional+de+Antropología+Mexico+City) · [Website](https://www.mna.inah.gob.mx/)

## Frida Kahlo Museum (Casa Azul)

Frida Kahlo's cobalt-blue birthplace and home in Coyoacán, now a museum of her life and work. Timed tickets, online only — book ahead (~250 MXN). Ticket also covers the nearby Anahuacalli Museum. Coyoacán itself is worth a half-day stroll.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Museo+Frida+Kahlo+Coyoacán+Mexico+City) · [Website](https://www.museofridakahlo.org.mx/)

## Teotihuacán

Ancient city ~50 km NE of CDMX (~1–1.5 hr) with the Pyramids of the Sun and Moon and the Avenue of the Dead. Easy self-guided day trip by bus from Terminal Central del Norte; plan 3–4 hrs on site, go early.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Teotihuacán+pyramids)

## Museo Soumaya

Carlos Slim's free museum in a striking silver Fernando Romero-designed building at Plaza Carso, Nuevo Polanco. 66,000+ works spanning 30 centuries — the largest Rodin collection outside France (The Thinker), plus Van Gogh, Dalí, and Mexican masters. Free, no ticket needed.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Museo+Soumaya+Plaza+Carso+Mexico+City) · [Website](https://www.museosoumaya.org/)

## Arena México

Home of CMLL lucha libre ("the Cathedral of Lucha Libre"). Masked wrestlers, big crowds, great atmosphere.

Schedule:

- **Tuesday** — *Martes Populares* (popular/cheaper night)
- **Friday** — *Viernes Espectacular*, the main event — **Friday night is the best experience**
- **Sunday** — family matinee
- **Saturday** — show moves to the smaller **Arena Coliseo** instead

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Arena+México+Mexico+City)

## Casa Izeba

Eight-room boutique guest house on Colima St in Roma Norte, behind a French-colonial façade — inner courtyard, terrace, and rooftop. Walkable to Rosetta and Máximo. Intimate, design-forward; good for couples.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Casa+Izeba+Roma+Norte+Mexico+City) · [Website](https://casaizeba.mx/)

## Buna

Well-loved specialty roaster (a.k.a. Buna - Café Rico) with a tight menu; signature Cafechata (cold-brew horchata). Small sidewalk seating on Orizaba near Plaza Río de Janeiro.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Buna+Café+Roma+Norte+Mexico+City)

## MOMO Coffee

Easygoing open-air specialty café on Jalapa (founded 2024); horchata latte, matcha, raspberry donut. Pet-friendly, good for working.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=MOMO+Coffee+Roma+Norte+Mexico+City)

## Cardinal Casa de Café

Specialty coffee known for Mexican beans and multiple brew methods; try the Voltaire (cappuccino with Oaxacan chocolate). Also a Condesa location.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Cardinal+Casa+de+Café+Roma+Norte+Mexico+City)

## Boicot Café

Artisanal coffee with retro/quirky decor (sofas, a Pac-Man machine); known for cold brew and nitro. Jalapa 99.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Boicot+Café+Roma+Norte+Mexico+City)

## Cafebrería El Péndulo

Bookstore-café (also bar/grill and small concert venue) on Álvaro Obregón — leafy, two-story, great for a relaxed coffee with a book.

[Map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Cafebrería+El+Péndulo+Roma+Mexico+City)
